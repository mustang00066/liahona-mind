=== THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (8-10 minutes) ===

--- Why Criticism Hurts ---

Austin, criticism hurts because we believe our worth depends on approval. But your worth is inherent. It cannot be decreased by criticism.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When you understand this, criticism becomes just words. Someone's opinion. It cannot change your fundamental value.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Types of Criticism ---

Learn to distinguish: constructive feedback is specific, from trustworthy sources, offered with care. This is valuable.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Destructive criticism is vague, mean-spirited, attacking your character rather than specific behaviors. This says more about the critic than about you.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Evaluation Process ---

When criticized, do not react immediately. Pause. Breathe. Then evaluate.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Consider the source. Is this person trustworthy? Look for specificity. Is this about behavior or vague character attack? Decide what is useful.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Useful feedback deserves a response. Destructive criticism deserves release. Let it go.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Identity Separation ---

"I made a mistake" is different from "I am a mistake." Criticism of behavior is not criticism of identity. Behavior can change. Worth remains constant.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Scripture Example ---

The Savior was criticized constantly. Yet He never wavered. He knew who He was. You can have this same security.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Direct Suggestions ---

From this day forward, criticism cannot devastate you. You hear it calmly. You evaluate it fairly.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When criticized, you pause before reacting. You create space between criticism and response.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You separate behavior from identity. You maintain your sense of self regardless of others' opinions.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Strengthening the Anchor ---

Press your thumb and ring finger together now.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Feel worth, trust, body, voice, and strengths flowing in. Add resilience to criticism. The calm evaluation that replaces emotional reaction.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Release. The anchor now carries six elements.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

--- Future Pacing ---

See yourself tomorrow receiving criticism. You pause, breathe, evaluate. You do not react emotionally.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

A week from now, this process is natural. You handle criticism with calm.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

A month from now, you benefit from criticism. Good feedback makes you better. Bad criticism bounces off.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

=== POST-HYPNOTIC SUGGESTIONS (1 minute) ===

Your resilience to criticism continues to grow. The evaluation process becomes automatic: pause, breathe, assess, respond.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Criticism cannot change who you are. Your worth is inherent and constant.
