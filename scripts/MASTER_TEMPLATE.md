# Liahona Mind - Hypnotherapy Session Master Template

## Research-Based Session Structure

Based on clinical hypnotherapy best practices from [ICCHP](https://icchp.com/free-hypnotherapy-training-course/six-6-stage-hypnotic-process), [UK College of Hypnosis](https://www.ukhypnosis.com/), [Silicon Valley Hypnosis Center](https://siliconvalleyhypnosiscenter.com/), and [Morpheus Clinic](https://www.morpheusclinic.com/stages-hypnosis-delving-deepeners/).

## DEEP SESSION TEMPLATE (35-40 minutes)
**Word count target: ~4,500-5,500 words** (at 130 wpm speaking pace)
**Total pause time: ~8-10 minutes**

### 1. PRE-TALK / SETTLING (2-3 minutes, ~300 words)
**Purpose:** Welcome, set expectations, grant permission to relax

**Elements:**
- Warm, personal welcome using [NAME]
- Acknowledge their journey/courage in seeking change
- Brief explanation of what to expect
- Permission statements ("You can relax completely...")
- Comfort instructions (headphones, position, environment)
- LDS-specific: Brief spiritual grounding, acknowledgment that healing is God's will

**Sample Pacing:**
```
[Opening greeting - 30 seconds]
[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]
[Permission to relax - 45 seconds]
[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]
[What to expect - 60 seconds]
[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]
[Spiritual grounding - 30 seconds]
```

---

### 2. INDUCTION (10-12 minutes, ~1,200 words)
**Purpose:** Guide into trance state, bypass critical faculty

**Choose ONE primary technique, layer with secondary:**

#### Option A: Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR)
- Start at top of head OR feet (traditionally head-down)
- Work through EVERY major muscle group with detailed attention
- Include: scalp, forehead, eyes, jaw, neck, shoulders, upper arms, forearms, hands, fingers, chest, stomach, lower back, hips, thighs, knees, calves, ankles, feet, toes
- Use "tense and release" OR "warm light spreading" metaphor
- Pacing: Each body area gets 15-20 seconds of attention

#### Option B: Breathing-Focused Induction
- 4-7-8 breathing pattern
- Count breaths (10 down to 1)
- Associate exhale with releasing tension
- "With each breath, going deeper..."

#### Option C: Eye Fixation + Closure
- Focus on a point (candle flame, spot on ceiling)
- Describe heaviness of eyelids
- Count down as eyes close naturally
- Transition to internal focus

#### Option D: Visualization-Based
- Safe place imagery from the start
- Detailed sensory engagement (see, hear, feel, smell)
- Natural deepening through the scene

**Layering:** Combine breathing suggestions throughout ANY induction type.

**Key Phrases:**
- "That's right..."
- "You're doing perfectly..."
- "Allow yourself to..."
- "Notice how..."
- "Deeper and deeper..."
- "More and more relaxed..."

**Pause Pattern:**
```
[Instruction/suggestion - 20-30 seconds]
[PAUSE 4-5 SECONDS]
[Next instruction - 20-30 seconds]
[PAUSE 4-5 SECONDS]
```

---

### 3. DEEPENING (8-10 minutes, ~900-1,100 words)
**Purpose:** Achieve optimal trance depth for suggestion work

**Use 2-3 deepening techniques in sequence:**

#### Technique 1: Staircase/Elevator Descent
- 20, 15, or 10 steps/floors
- Each step = deeper relaxation
- Describe the environment (marble stairs, soft carpet, golden light)
- Number slowly with suggestions between

```
Twenty... stepping down, going deeper...
[PAUSE 2 SECONDS]
Nineteen... more relaxed with every step...
[PAUSE 2 SECONDS]
```

#### Technique 2: Counting Down
- Count from 10 to 1 (or 5 to 1)
- Doubling relaxation with each number
- "With each number, twice as relaxed as before..."

#### Technique 3: Safe Place Elaboration
- Expand the safe place visualization
- Add sensory details progressively
- Anchor the feeling of safety

#### Technique 4: Fractionation
- "In a moment, I'll ask you to open your eyes briefly..."
- Brief eye opening (3-5 seconds)
- Close again, go twice as deep
- Repeat 2-3 times

#### Technique 5: Body Scan Deepener
- Quick scan from head to toe
- Notice and release any remaining tension
- Confirm total relaxation

**Depth Testing Phrases:**
- "Notice how deeply relaxed you've become..."
- "Your subconscious mind is now fully open and receptive..."
- "You're in the perfect state for positive change..."

---

### 4. THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (15-18 minutes, ~1,800-2,200 words)
**Purpose:** Deliver issue-specific suggestions, metaphors, and change work

**Structure within this section:**

#### 4A. Issue Acknowledgment (1-2 min)
- Acknowledge the struggle without judgment
- Validate their experience
- Reframe the issue (not a flaw, but a pattern that can change)
- LDS: God's love is unconditional, change is possible

#### 4B. Root Cause Exploration (2-3 min)
- Gentle exploration of when pattern started
- Understanding the positive intention behind the behavior
- Permission to release old patterns
- "Your subconscious was trying to protect/help you..."

#### 4C. Core Therapeutic Metaphor (4-5 min)
- Extended metaphor relevant to the issue
- **Anxiety:** Storm passing, anchor in the harbor, mountain stream
- **Phone Addiction:** Breaking chains, reclaiming your garden, turning off the noise machine
- **Confidence:** Diamond covered in mud, emerging butterfly, standing tall as a tree
- **Overthinking:** Snow globe settling, river finding its course, clouds passing
- **Nail Biting:** Hands becoming precious instruments, protective barrier around nails
- **Pornography:** Cleaning a window to see clearly, breaking free from chains, the prodigal son returning
- **Family Presence:** Coming home to yourself, the lighthouse for your family

Metaphor should have:
- Beginning state (problem)
- Transformation process
- End state (resolution)
- Emotional resonance

#### 4D. Direct Suggestions (4-5 min)
- Specific behavioral suggestions
- Positive framing (what they WILL do, not won't do)
- Repetition of key suggestions (3x minimum)
- Graduated suggestions (easy â†’ challenging)
- Future pacing ("Tomorrow when you wake...")

**Suggestion Formula:**
1. State the suggestion positively
2. Describe how it feels
3. Connect to their values/identity
4. Repeat in slightly different words

#### 4E. Anchoring (2-3 min)
- Create a physical trigger (thumb-finger press, hand on heart, deep breath)
- Associate with the positive state achieved
- Practice activating the anchor
- "Whenever you [anchor], these feelings return instantly..."

#### 4F. Future Pacing / Mental Rehearsal (2-3 min)
- Guide them through future scenarios with new behavior
- Visualize success in specific situations
- Feel the emotions of having already changed
- "See yourself a week from now... a month... a year..."

---

### 5. POST-HYPNOTIC SUGGESTIONS (2-3 minutes, ~300 words)
**Purpose:** Bridge trance state to daily life

**Elements:**
- Triggers for accessing trance/calm state in daily life
- Reinforcement instructions (listen to this session again, practice anchor)
- "Each time you listen, the suggestions become stronger..."
- Sleep suggestions if appropriate (for evening sessions)
- Permission to remember what's helpful, forget what isn't
- LDS: Prayer and scripture as reinforcement

---

### 6. EMERGENCE (3-5 minutes, ~400 words)
**Purpose:** Gradual return to full alertness

**Structure:**
```
[Preparation - signal that session is ending]
[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

[Count up from 1-5 or 1-10]
- 1: "Beginning to return, feeling peaceful..."
- 2: "Becoming more aware of your body..."
- 3: "Energy returning, feeling refreshed..."
- 4: "Almost fully alert, feeling wonderful..."
- 5: "Eyes open, fully awake, feeling better than before..."

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

[Positive closing affirmations]
[Final blessing/well-wishes]
[Grounding instructions - wiggle fingers, stretch]
```

**Key Emergence Elements:**
- Gradual (never rush)
- Positive suggestions for how they'll feel
- Grounding in body sensations
- Affirmation of the work done
- Gentle return to full alertness

## QUICK SESSION TEMPLATE (18-22 minutes)
**Word count target: ~2,200-2,800 words**
**Total pause time: ~4-5 minutes**

### Condensed Structure:

| Section | Time | Words |
|---------|------|-------|
| Pre-talk/Settling | 1-2 min | 150-200 |
| Induction | 5-6 min | 600-700 |
| Deepening | 3-4 min | 400-500 |
| Therapeutic Content | 7-9 min | 850-1,100 |
| Post-Hypnotic | 1 min | 100-150 |
| Emergence | 2-3 min | 250-300 |

### Quick Session Modifications:
- Shorter induction using RAPID technique (combine PMR with breathing)
- Single deepening technique (staircase OR counting, not both)
- Condensed therapeutic content:
  - Brief acknowledgment
  - ONE core metaphor (shorter version)
  - Key direct suggestions (most important only)
  - Quick anchor installation
  - Brief future pacing
- Faster emergence (5-count instead of 10)

## LDS-SPECIFIC ELEMENTS

Incorporate throughout (not forced, natural):

- **Scriptural references:** Peace passages (D&C 6:36, John 14:27, Philippians 4:6-7)
- **Atonement themes:** Christ's healing power, grace for change
- **Temple imagery:** Sacred spaces, purity, light
- **Covenant identity:** Who they really are as children of God
- **Prayer connection:** Sessions as a form of communion with God
- **Faith framing:** Change through faith AND works (effort + grace)

**Avoid:**
- Preachiness or lecturing
- Guilt-inducing language
- Overly specific doctrine that might alienate
- Anything that contradicts relaxation

---

## VOICE/DELIVERY NOTES

For text-to-speech generation:
- Use ellipses (...) to create natural pauses within sentences
- Write in a slow, measured rhythm
- Shorter sentences for impact
- Vary sentence length for natural flow
- Use "you" frequently for direct connection
- Permissive language: "allow yourself to...", "you might notice...", "perhaps you'll find..."
