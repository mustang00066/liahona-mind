--- Exploring the Emotional Roots ---

Austin, every habit exists for a reason. At some point, nail biting began serving a purpose—helping you manage something that felt difficult. This was not weakness. It was your brain trying to help you cope.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Let us explore the emotions that may connect to your nail biting.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Anxiety is the most common driver. When we feel worried, energy builds in the body. The hands seek something to do. Biting provides movement and a brief sense of control.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Does anxiety connect to your nail biting? Let yourself notice without judgment.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Boredom is another common driver. When the mind is not engaged, the hands crave activity. Nail biting fills the void.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Does boredom connect? In quiet moments, while watching television? Let yourself notice.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Frustration and anger sometimes trigger biting. When we cannot express irritation, biting provides physical release.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Sadness and loneliness can also drive the habit. The repetitive motion provides self-soothing, comfort when no other comfort is available.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Some bite due to perfectionism about the nails—needing to "fix" a rough edge, but making it worse.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Compassion for Your Coping ---

Whatever emotions you've discovered, approach them with compassion. You learned to cope in the best way you knew how. The habit developed because something inside you needed help.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The Savior was a man of sorrows and acquainted with grief. He understands emotional pain. He does not judge you.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Now, with more wisdom and tools, you can meet those emotional needs in better ways.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Healthier Ways to Meet the Need ---

When you feel anxious, you have options. Take three deep breaths. Activate your anchor. Step outside. Pray. Anxiety does not require biting—it requires acknowledgment and release.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When you feel bored, you have options. Move your body. Engage your mind. Use the moment for reflection. Boredom does not require biting—it requires engagement.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When you feel frustrated, you have options. Clench your fists and release. Express the feeling in words. Exercise. Frustration does not require biting—it requires expression.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When you feel sad, you have options. Reach out to someone. Pray. Journal. Sadness does not require biting—it requires connection.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When you notice an imperfect nail, use a file. Smooth the edge properly. Remind yourself: imperfect nails are fine. They will heal.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Feelings Are Temporary ---

Here is a fundamental truth: all feelings are temporary. They rise, peak, and fall. No feeling lasts forever.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Research shows emotional surges pass within ninety seconds to three minutes. You can outlast any feeling.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

See yourself feeling a difficult emotion. You notice it. Name it. Watch it. It builds, peaks, fades. Your hands never moved. The feeling passed on its own. You are stronger than you knew.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]
