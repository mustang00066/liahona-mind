=== THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (8-10 minutes) ===

--- Understanding Nail Biting ---

Austin, nail biting is a body-focused repetitive behavior. It is not a character flaw or weakness. It is simply a pattern your brain learned as a way to cope with certain feelings or situations.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Your brain is good at learning patterns. When something provides even a small sense of relief, the brain records it: "This helps. Do this again." Over time, it becomes automatic. You may not realize you're doing it until you're already biting.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

This automatic nature is why willpower alone often fails. The behavior lives in the subconscious mind. That's why hypnotherapy is so effective—it speaks directly to where the pattern lives.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Habit Loop ---

All habits follow a three-part cycle: trigger, routine, and reward.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Triggers can be external (situations, environments) or internal (emotions, sensations). Common triggers include: anxiety, boredom, concentration, watching television, perfectionism about the nails themselves.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

The routine is the behavior itself—hands moving toward mouth, teeth finding nail.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The reward is whatever benefit the brain perceives: reduced anxiety, relief from boredom, the satisfaction of "fixing" a rough nail.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

To break the habit, we address all three parts.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Power of Awareness ---

The most important skill you will develop is awareness—noticing when you're biting or about to bite.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Through this work, you will develop "early warning detection." You will notice the urge before it leads to action—the restlessness in your hands, the thoughts about your nails, the drift of your hand toward your face.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

This awareness creates a gap where choice becomes possible. Awareness is power.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]
