habits like nail biting often serve an emotional purpose. They may have started as a way to cope with feelings that felt overwhelming. Understanding this is not about blame, but about compassion and healing.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

In the safety of this hypnotic state, you can explore these roots gently. Your subconscious mind knows the origins. It remembers what you may have forgotten. And it is ready to help you heal.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Sometimes nail biting connects to anxiety. A feeling of worry or nervousness that seeks an outlet. The repetitive motion soothes the anxious mind temporarily, giving it something to focus on.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

If anxiety has been a companion in your life, know that there are better ways to soothe it. Ways that don't damage your body. Ways that actually address the worry rather than just distracting from it.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Sometimes nail biting connects to perfectionism or self-criticism. A feeling that you are not good enough, that you must constantly fix or correct something about yourself. The nails become a target for this critical energy.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

But you are enough. Right now, as you are. The Savior does not require perfection for His love. He loves you in your imperfection and invites you to grow, but His love is unconditional.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Let that truth sink into your heart. You are loved. You are worthy. You do not need to pick and bite at yourself, literally or figuratively. Self-compassion is the healing path.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Sometimes nail biting connects to control. When life feels chaotic or overwhelming, there can be a subconscious desire to control something, anything. And the nails, part of your own body, become that thing.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

But the need for control often comes from fear. Fear that things will fall apart. Fear that you cannot handle what comes. The truth is, Austin, that you don't need to control everything. You only need to trust the One who does.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The scripture says, Trust in the Lord with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding. As you release the need for control, the need to bite decreases. You can let go, because God has hold of you.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 8 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]
