=== THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (8-10 minutes) ===

--- The Competing Response ---

Austin, the competing response is the core technique of Habit Reversal Training. It is beautifully simple: when an urge to bite arises, you engage in a different physical action that makes biting impossible.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Your competing response is making a gentle fist. When you notice an urge, you immediately form both hands into soft fists and hold until the urge passes.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Not tense fists. Not white-knuckle gripping. Just gentle, soft fists. Fingers curled toward palms. Thumbs resting lightly outside the fingers. Comfortable enough to hold for several minutes.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Why the Fist Works ---

Let us understand why this simple technique is so powerful.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

First, it makes biting physically impossible. Your fingers are curled. They cannot reach your mouth. The automatic pathway is blocked.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Second, it gives the urge time to pass. Urges build, peak, and fade. They rarely last more than two to three minutes. By holding the fist, you ride out the wave.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Third, it engages your hands in a deliberate action. This satisfies the body's desire to do something. The restlessness finds an outlet.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Fourth, with repetition, it rewires your brain. Each time you use the competing response, the neural pathway from urge to fist gets stronger. The old pathway gets weaker.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Fifth, it is subtle and socially acceptable. You can make gentle fists anywhere—at work, in conversation, in public. No one notices.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Installing the Response ---

Let us install this competing response now.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

In your mind, feel yourself making gentle fists. Fingers curling toward palms. Thumbs resting outside. Soft, not tense. Comfortable.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

This is what your hands will do whenever an urge arises. Urge equals fist. Automatic. No thought required.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]
