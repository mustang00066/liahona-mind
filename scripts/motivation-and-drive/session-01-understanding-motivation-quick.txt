=== THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (8-10 minutes) ===

--- What Motivation Is ---

Motivation is the energy that moves you toward action. It is not something you wait for—it is something you generate.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Understanding how to generate motivation changes everything. You become the author of your actions.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Intrinsic Versus Extrinsic ---

Extrinsic motivation comes from external rewards—money, recognition, avoiding punishment. It has limitations.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Intrinsic motivation comes from within—interest, values, meaning. Research shows it is more sustainable and satisfying.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

This series develops intrinsic motivation by connecting your actions to your deeper purpose.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Science ---

Dopamine is the motivation molecule. It is released when you anticipate something good, driving you toward your goal.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Clear, compelling goals create dopamine. Small wins create dopamine. This is why clarity and momentum matter.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Motivation Myth ---

Many believe they need to feel motivated before acting. This is backwards.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Action often creates motivation. When you start, momentum builds. Energy appears. You become motivated through the doing.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Stop waiting. Start acting.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Role of Purpose ---

The deepest motivation comes from purpose—knowing WHY you are doing something.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Purpose provides direction, resilience, and meaning. When connected to purpose, effort becomes offering.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Installing the Anchor ---

Make a fist with your dominant hand. Firm but comfortable. Feel the power.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

This fist represents your inner drive—your power to act, to move forward. Say internally: "I have the power to act. I am driven."

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Release your fist.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]
