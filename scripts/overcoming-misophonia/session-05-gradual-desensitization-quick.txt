=== THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (8-10 minutes) ===

Receive only what serves you. Your subconscious mind will accept what is true for you and let the rest go.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

--- How Desensitization Works ---

When you experience a trigger WITHOUT the stress response, your brain learns it is not dangerous. The overreactive pathway weakens. A calm pathway strengthens.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Your Safe Space ---

Imagine a place where you feel completely safe. A beach, forest, or room. See it clearly. This is your retreat. You can return here anytime.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Distance Technique ---

Imagine a very large room. You are at one end. At the far end, barely visible, is someone making a trigger sound.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

At this distance, it is faint, almost imperceptible. Your body remains calm. Breathe. Press your anchor. "This is just a sound. I am safe."

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Moving Closer ---

Take a few steps closer. The sound is slightly louder but still faint.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Check your body. If tension arises, breathe. Anchor. Reframe: "Uncomfortable but safe."

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You remain calm. Your brain is learning.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Returning to Safety ---

Step back to the far end. The sound becomes distant. You can always retreat. Desensitization is gentle stretching, then rest.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Volume Dial ---

Imagine a dial controlling the trigger's volume. It is at 2 out of 10. Very quiet.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Turn it to 3. Slightly louder. Breathe. Anchor. You remain calm.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Turn back to 2. You control the intensity.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Anchor Reinforcement ---

Press thumb into palm, reinforcing your anchor.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

This anchor carries desensitization. It helps you approach triggers while calm.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Say internally: "My brain is rewiring. Each practice makes me stronger."

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Release your thumb.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]
