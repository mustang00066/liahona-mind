--- What Is Misophonia ---

Misophonia means "hatred of sound." But it is more accurately a strong emotional and physical reaction to specific trigger sounds.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Common triggers include chewing, breathing, sniffing, clicking, and certain vocal sounds. The reaction feels automatic—the sound occurs and within milliseconds, distress floods your body.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You are not crazy. You are not overreacting. Your brain has learned to treat certain sounds as threats.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Brain Science ---

Research shows that in people with misophonia, trigger sounds take a fast track to the amygdala—the brain's threat center.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Your amygdala triggers fight-or-flight: adrenaline, increased heart rate, anger, the need to escape. This is why you cannot simply "ignore it."

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

But here is the good news: what the brain learned, the brain can unlearn. Neural pathways can be rewired.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Misophonia Cycle ---

The cycle begins with the trigger sound. Your brain detects it and activates threat responses.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Then comes the emotional reaction: anger, anxiety, disgust. Then the behavioral response: fleeing, glaring, snapping.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Often guilt and shame follow. You suffer from the trigger and from your reaction to it. This series addresses both layers.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Path Forward ---

In this series, you will develop awareness of triggers and early warning signs. You will learn to calm your nervous system.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You will change your thoughts about trigger sounds. Through gradual exposure, you will desensitize. You will establish a new identity—someone at peace with sound.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Installing the Anchor ---

Take your right thumb and press it gently but firmly into the center of your left palm.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

This gesture becomes your calm anchor. Feel the safety. Feel how calm you are right now. This feeling is being connected to this gesture.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Say internally: "I am safe. Sounds cannot harm me. I am in control of my response."

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Release your thumb.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]
