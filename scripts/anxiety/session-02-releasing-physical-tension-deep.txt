[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 6 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

--- Recognizing Physical Anxiety Signals ---

Anxiety sends physical signals before it fully takes hold. Learning to recognize these early warning signs gives you power. You can intervene before anxiety builds momentum.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Common signals include: tension in the shoulders and neck... a clenched jaw... shallow breathing from the chest... a knot or butterflies in the stomach... racing heart... sweaty palms... and a general sense of physical unease.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

From now on, you become more aware of these signals in your body. When you notice any of them, it's simply information. Your body telling you: "Pay attention. Something needs releasing."

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Quick Body Scan ---

I'm going to teach you a rapid body scan you can do anywhere, anytime. It takes only seconds, but its power is immense.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

The scan has three checkpoints: shoulders, jaw, and breath. These are the three most common places tension hides... and the three easiest to release quickly.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

First, shoulders. Several times throughout your day, you'll simply notice: Are my shoulders raised? Are they tight? And if so, you'll consciously drop them. Let them fall. Release.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Second, jaw. Is your jaw clenched? Are your teeth pressed together? Simply let your jaw hang loose. Let your teeth part. Feel instant relief.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Third, breath. Are you breathing shallowly? From your chest only? Take one deep belly breath. Feel your abdomen expand. Exhale slowly. Your nervous system resets.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Shoulders. Jaw. Breath. Three seconds. That's all it takes. And in those three seconds, you interrupt the anxiety cycle before it gains power.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

This is your new habit. Shoulders. Jaw. Breath. Many times each day, automatically checking, automatically releasing. Tension never builds. Anxiety never gains foothold.

--- The 4-4-4 Breathing Technique ---

Your breath is perhaps your most powerful tool. It's the one system in your body that's both automatic AND under your conscious control. This gives you a direct line to your nervous system.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When you breathe slowly and deeply, you activate your parasympathetic nervous system... the "rest and digest" system. Your heart rate slows. Your blood pressure drops. Your muscles relax. Your body receives the message: "All is well. We are safe."

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

The 4-4-4 breathing pattern is simple and powerful. You breathe in for four counts. You hold for four counts. You breathe out for four counts. This rhythm naturally calms your entire system.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Let's practice together. Breathe in... two... three... four.

[PAUSE 1 SECOND]

[PAUSE 1 SECOND]

[PAUSE 1 SECOND]

[PAUSE 1 SECOND]

[PAUSE 1 SECOND]

[PAUSE 1 SECOND]

Breathe out... two... three... four.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]
