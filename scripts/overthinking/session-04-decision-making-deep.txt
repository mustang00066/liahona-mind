[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

You are in a place of clear-minded peace. From here, decisions feel simple. Not because they are, but because you are capable.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Decision paralysis comes from the belief that there is one perfect choice, and you must find it. But this belief is false. Most decisions have multiple good options.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The overthinking mind analyzes endlessly, hoping for certainty. But certainty rarely comes. Waiting for certainty is really refusing to decide.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Here is a liberating truth: in most decisions, any reasonable choice is better than no choice. The cost of indecision often exceeds the cost of an imperfect decision.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The scripture tells us, If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God. Then it adds something crucial: ask in faith, nothing wavering. Faith means deciding before you have all the answers.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You can make good decisions with incomplete information. You do it every day. You drive without knowing every car's movement. You speak without scripting every word. You trust the process.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Let us create a decision process. When facing a choice, first clarify: What am I actually deciding? Often indecision comes from unclear framing.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Second, gather reasonable information. Notice the word reasonable. Not perfect information. Not complete information. Just enough to decide thoughtfully.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Third, consider your options. Usually there are two to four real choices. More than that is often analysis paralysis.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Fourth, notice which option your gut favors. Your intuition has processed more than your conscious mind can. Trust it as data.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Fifth, decide. Pick an option. Say: I choose this. Feel the relief that comes from choosing.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Sixth, commit. Once you decide, stop analyzing. The decision is made. Move forward.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

If the decision does not work out, you can adjust. Decisions are rarely permanent. You can course-correct. This reduces the pressure.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

From now on, you will use this process. Clarify. Gather reasonable information. Consider options. Notice your gut. Decide. Commit. Six steps to freedom from paralysis.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Let us practice now. Think of a decision you have been avoiding. Nothing huge, just something waiting for your choice.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

What are you actually deciding? Clarify it now.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

What are your options? Usually two or three.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

What does your gut say? Which feels right?

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Now, decide. In your mind, say: I choose this. Feel the relief.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Good. You just made a decision. It was not that hard. You are capable.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Rest in this clarity for a moment. You are someone who can decide. Not perfectly, but well enough. That is all you need.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]
