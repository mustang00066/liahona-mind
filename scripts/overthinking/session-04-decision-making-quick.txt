=== THERAPEUTIC CONTENT (8-10 minutes) ===

--- Understanding Decision Paralysis ---

At the root of paralysis is a false belief: that one perfect choice exists. But in most decisions, there is no perfect choice. There are multiple good options, each with trade-offs.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

The cost of indecision is underestimated. While you deliberate, opportunities close. Any reasonable choice is usually better than continued paralysis.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- The Six-Step Process ---

Let me teach you a simple process for deciding without endless analysis.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Step One: Clarify what you're actually deciding. Define the specific choice.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Step Two: Gather reasonable information. Not perfect. Not complete. Just enough.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Step Three: Identify your options. Usually two to four real choices.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Step Four: Notice your gut response. Your intuition has wisdom. Trust it as data.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Step Five: Decide. Say clearly: "I choose this." Feel the relief.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Step Six: Commit. Stop analyzing. Move forward with your choice.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Six steps: Clarify. Gather reasonably. Options. Gut. Decide. Commit.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Good Enough ---

Learn to recognize "good enough." A decision is good enough when it reasonably addresses the situation and aligns with your values. It doesn't need to be perfect.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Direct Suggestions ---
