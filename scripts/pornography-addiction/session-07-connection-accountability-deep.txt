Austin, there is a saying in recovery: We are only as sick as our secrets. The addiction has likely lived in secrecy, hidden from those who love you. This secrecy has given it power.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When we bring struggles into the light, shame loses its grip. When another human knows our battle and still accepts us, we begin to heal. This is the power of connection.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The Savior modeled this. He did not withdraw from those who struggled. He drew near to sinners, loved them. His presence transformed them.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You need at least one person who knows the full truth of your struggle. A spouse, trusted friend, priesthood leader, therapist, or recovery sponsor.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

This person is your accountability partner. Someone you can call when temptation is strong. Someone who checks in. Someone who celebrates victories and lifts you after defeats.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

If you don't have such a person, make finding one a priority. Pray for guidance. The Lord will lead you to the right person.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

In your imagination, see yourself reaching out to this person. Being honest about your struggle.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

It takes courage. Your heart pounds. But you speak the truth. Instead of rejection, you receive acceptance. They see you as brave for being honest.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Feel the relief of that honesty. The weight you've carried alone is now shared. Someone knows, and they still care. This is the beginning of freedom.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

Accountability is practical. Regular check-ins keep you honest. Knowing you will report to someone adds weight to choices. It is easier to say no when you know you will tell someone.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Consider establishing a routine. Perhaps a daily text. Perhaps a weekly call. Regularity matters.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

When tempted, reach out before acting. A simple text: I am struggling right now. Just sending that can break the spell of temptation.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

If you fall, tell them quickly. Not days later. Quick confession prevents shame from compounding. It keeps you in the light.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Support groups can be powerful. Programs like the Church's Addiction Recovery Program provide community. You discover you are not uniquely broken. Others share your struggle. Others have found freedom.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

The Lord organized His Church as a community. We need each other. Bearing one another's burdens is part of the covenant. Let others help bear yours.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Isolation is a lie from the adversary. He wants you to believe you must struggle alone, that you would be rejected if known. These are lies.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Vulnerability creates connection. When you are honest, others often share their struggles. You create space for authentic relationship.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Imagine yourself in the future. You have a network of support. An accountability partner who knows everything. You are no longer alone.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

You live in the light, and the light sustains your recovery. Connected. Supported. Known. Free.
