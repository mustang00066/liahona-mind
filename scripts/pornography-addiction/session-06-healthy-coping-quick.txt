--- Why We Need Healthy Coping ---

Austin, here is a fundamental truth: we all need ways to cope with life's challenges.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Stress, boredom, loneliness, sadness, anxiety, frustration—these are part of being human. We need ways to process these experiences.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Emotional Coping ---

The first category is emotional coping—learning to process feelings directly.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

The most powerful emotional coping skill: feel the feeling. Most of us try to escape emotions. But emotions are meant to be felt. When you allow a feeling to exist, it processes naturally and passes.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Name what you feel. Say: "I feel sad. I feel anxious. I feel lonely." Naming emotions activates your prefrontal cortex and reduces the intensity of the feeling.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Journaling externalizes emotions. Write what you are feeling. Get it out of your head and onto paper. This creates clarity and releases pressure.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Prayer processes emotions while connecting you to divine help. Pour out your heart to your Father in Heaven. He listens. He responds. He sends peace.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Physical Coping ---

The second category is physical coping—using your body to regulate your nervous system.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Exercise is one of the most powerful coping tools available. It releases endorphins, burns stress hormones, and provides healthy dopamine. Walk, run, do pushups, lift weights. Physical movement breaks mental spirals.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Cold water is remarkably effective. Splash cold water on your face. Take a cold shower. Cold activates your vagus nerve and resets your nervous system.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Sleep is coping. Remember HALT—when you are Hungry, Angry, Lonely, or Tired, you are vulnerable. When tired, rest. This is not weakness—it is wisdom.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Breathwork is available anytime. Box breathing: inhale for four, hold for four, exhale for four, hold for four. This calms your nervous system within minutes.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Social Coping ---

The third category is social coping—connection with others.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Isolation breeds addiction. Connection heals it. When you feel the pull toward escape, reach out to someone instead.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Call a friend or family member. Send a text. Have a real conversation. Human connection provides the genuine intimacy that pornography counterfeits.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Service to others is powerful coping. When you help someone else, you step outside your own struggles. Serving others releases oxytocin and creates meaning.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Spiritual Coping ---

The fourth category is spiritual coping—drawing on divine resources.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Scripture study brings peace that the world cannot give. Even five minutes with the word of God can shift your entire state.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Temple attendance creates a space apart from the world. The temple is a place of healing and revelation.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Worship music can change your emotional state. Create a playlist of hymns or spiritual songs that lift you.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Remember: your Heavenly Father has infinite resources available. Prayer opens the channel to receive them.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]

--- Cognitive Coping ---

The fifth category is cognitive coping—changing how you think.

[PAUSE 3 SECONDS]

Question negative thoughts. When your mind says, "This is unbearable," ask: "Is this thought true? Can I survive this feeling?" The answer is always yes.

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Reframe challenges as growth opportunities. Instead of "This is too hard," try "This is making me stronger."

[PAUSE 4 SECONDS]

Use the STOP technique from Session Four. Stop, Take a breath, Observe, and Proceed mindfully. This creates space for wise choices.

[PAUSE 5 SECONDS]
